GMAT Exam (Focus Edition) Overview

The GMAT Exam (Focus Edition) is the latest version designed to assess your skills in Quantitative Reasoning, Verbal Reasoning, and Data Insights. With a total score range of 205–805 (all scores ending in 5), the exam now equally weights all three sections.

Note: This edition excludes Sentence Correction and Geometry questions while emphasizing Multi-Source Reasoning to measure analytical and problem-solving skills.

Exam Sections

Quantitative Reasoning

21 Problem Solving questions in 45 minutes. This section tests your ability to apply arithmetic and algebraic reasoning to solve problems.

Score Range: 60 – 90
Verbal Reasoning

23 Reading Comprehension and Critical Reasoning questions in 45 minutes. This section evaluates your reading and argument evaluation skills.

Score Range: 60 – 90
Data Insights

20 questions in 45 minutes, including Data Sufficiency, Multi-Source Reasoning, Table Analysis, Graphics Interpretation, and Two-Part Analysis. An on-screen calculator is provided.

Score Range: 60 – 90

Scoring Techniques

The GMAT Exam (Focus Edition) Total Score is calculated by equally weighting the three sections: Quantitative Reasoning, Verbal Reasoning, and Data Insights. Scores are based on:

  • Number of questions answered
  • Correctness of answers
  • Difficulty and other parameters of the questions

Percentile rankings, based on the previous three years of exam data, help you gauge performance relative to other candidates.
